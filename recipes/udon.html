Kitsune Udon

Description

Allegedly the most popular udon dish in Japan and named after a fox of Japanese mythology, Kitsune Udon is umami on top of umami with shiitake mushroom soaked in dashi stock and imbued with usukuchi soy sauce. Although this is the most complicated dish on this website, there's nothing here that wouldn't be too difficult to get a handle on.Original recipe from norecipes.com

Ingredients

For udon soup

  • 1 1/2 cups of water
  • 4 grams konbu (a type of dried kelp)
  • 3 grams dried shiitake mushroom
  • 2 teaspoons usukuchi soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

For Inari Agé

  • 1 aburaage
  • 1 tablespoon evaporated cane sugar
  • 2 tablespoons sake
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 1 tablespoon usukuchi soy sauce

Steps

  1. Rehydrate Add your konbu and dried shiitake to a 1 1/2 cup bowl of cold water. Allow them to rehydrate fully, which could take 30-60 minutes
  2. Boil, boil, boil Bring a pan of water to a boil then add your aburaage. Boil until the water becomes cloudy which take about a minute.
  3. Drain! Drain the water and clean your pan. Return to your aburaage and slice it in half
  4. Flavorize it In your pan, add your sugar, sake, water, and soy sauce and bring this mixture to a boil. It's now time for the aburaage to make its big comeback to pan town. Place both halves in the mixture. Spruce things up with the stem from the shiitake if it has finished rehydrating.
  5. Flip then flip then Keep flipping until there is no liquid left. Set aside your Inari Agé; it's time to move on to the udon.
  6. Begin the broth Remove the hydrated shiitake and konbu from the bowl and pour the water through a fine-mesh strainer into a saucepan.
  7. Mature the broth Add soy sauce, sugar, and salt to the saucepan, then bring to a boil. Skim off any excess foam that appears at the top
  8. Plate the udon Pour the soup into the bowl first. Then add in your boiled and strained udon noodles (preparation of such not included in this recipe guide). Top it all off with the Inari Agé, scallions, yuzu zest, maybe even your shiitake if you'd like.
